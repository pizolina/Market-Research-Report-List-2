<p><p>A gravity-based water purifier is a type of water filtration system that uses the force of gravity to remove impurities from water. It operates on the principle of downward filtration, where water is poured into an upper chamber and then passes through multiple layers of filters before reaching a lower chamber, where clean water is collected. These filters can include activated carbon, ceramic, and sediment filters, which effectively remove contaminants such as bacteria, viruses, protozoa, and suspended particles.</p><p>The gravity-based water purifier market has been experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing concerns regarding waterborne diseases and the need for clean and safe drinking water. Growing population, rapid urbanization, and industrialization are some of the key factors contributing to the market growth. Additionally, the increasing awareness about the importance of clean water and the rise in disposable income have also boosted the demand for gravity-based water purifiers.</p><p>In terms of market analysis, the gravity-based water purifier market is segmented by end-users, including residential, commercial, and industrial sectors. The residential sector holds the largest market share due to the widespread adoption of these purifiers in households. The commercial and industrial sectors are also witnessing substantial growth as organizations prioritize providing clean water to their employees and customers.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the gravity-based water purifier market include the introduction of advanced filtration technologies, such as UV and UF filters, to enhance the purification process. Additionally, the integration of smart features and the use of IoT (Internet of Things) for water quality monitoring and filter replacement notifications are gaining traction in the market. These technological advancements are expected to drive market growth and provide lucrative opportunities for manufacturers.</p><p>Overall, the gravity-based water purifier market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 13.9% during the forecast period. Factors such as increasing awareness about waterborne diseases, rising disposable income, and advancements in water purification technologies are expected to drive the market further.</p></p>
