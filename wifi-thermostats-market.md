<p><p>WiFi thermostats come in two main market types: battery-powered and hardwired. Battery-powered thermostats operate using replaceable batteries, making them more flexible in terms of installation locations as they don't require a direct electrical connection. On the other hand, hardwired thermostats are connected to the home's electrical system and do not rely on batteries. This type of thermostat provides a continuous power source and eliminates the need for battery replacements. Both types offer the convenience of wireless connectivity to control heating and cooling systems remotely via WiFi technology.</p></p>
