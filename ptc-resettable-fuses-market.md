<p><p>The PTC Resettable Fuses market includes two types: CPTC (Current-Protecting Polyswitch Devices) and PPTC (Polymeric Positive Temperature Coefficient Devices). CPTC fuses protect electronic circuits by rapidly generating high resistance when a fault occurs, limiting the current flow. On the other hand, PPTC fuses rely on a polymeric material that exhibits resistance increase as temperature rises. Once a fault occurs, their resistance quickly increases, limiting the current flow and protecting the circuit. Both types of fuses are resettable, meaning they return to their normal state after the fault is cleared.</p></p>
