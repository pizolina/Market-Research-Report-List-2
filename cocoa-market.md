<p><p>The cocoa market consists of three primary segments: cocoa liquor, cocoa butter, and cocoa powder. Cocoa liquor is produced by grinding cocoa beans into a paste, which is then further processed to separate cocoa butter and cocoa powder. Cocoa butter is the fatty component of the cocoa bean and is widely used in the production of chocolate and cosmetics. Cocoa powder is the non-fat component of the cocoa bean and is commonly used in baking and as a chocolate flavoring. These three segments play distinct roles in the chocolate and food industries.</p></p>
