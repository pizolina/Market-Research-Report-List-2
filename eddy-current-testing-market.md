<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Eddy Current Testing (ECT) is a non-destructive testing method used to detect defects or irregularities in conductive materials. It has different market types. Conventional ECT uses a probe to induce eddy currents and detect defects on the surface. Alternating Current Field Measurement (ACFM) measures the amplitude and phase of the alternating current field near a defect. Remote Field Testing (RFT) detects defects in ferromagnetic tubing. Eddy Current Array (ECA) uses multiple sensors to inspect larger areas efficiently. Pulsed Eddy Current Testing measures the decay rate of eddy currents to identify hidden corrosion. Near-Field Testing (NFT) and Near-Field Array (NFA) inspect non-magnetic materials. Partial Saturation Eddy Current (PSEC) detects flaws using saturation currents from a permanent magnet.</p></p>
