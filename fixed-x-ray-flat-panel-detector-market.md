<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Fixed X-Ray Flat Panel Detector Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Direct Conversion</li><li>Indirect Conversion</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Fixed X-Ray Flat Panel Detector market is categorized into two types: Direct Conversion and Indirect Conversion. </p><p>Direct Conversion detectors directly convert X-ray photons into electronic signals without the need for scintillators, resulting in high image quality with minimal noise. They are commonly used in applications where high-resolution imaging is required, such as radiography and mammography.</p><p>Indirect Conversion detectors, on the other hand, use scintillators to convert X-ray photons into visible light, which is then converted into electronic signals. These detectors are less expensive but may introduce some noise and image degradation due to the scintillator's output. They are commonly used in general radiography and fluoroscopy applications.</p></p>
