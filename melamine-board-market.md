<p><ul><li>Melamine Faced Chipboard</li><li>Medium Density Fiberboard</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The melamine board market consists of two main types - melamine faced chipboard and medium density fiberboard (MDF). Melamine faced chipboard is made by bonding a layer of melamine-impregnated paper to a chipboard panel, resulting in a durable and affordable surface for furniture, shelving, and cabinetry. On the other hand, MDF is produced by compressing wood fibers with a resin binder, creating a dense and smooth board with excellent strength and versatility. Both types find widespread use in the construction industry due to their durability and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
