<p><p>Baby clinical nutrition market includes three types of administration methods: oral administration, enteral administration, and intravenous administration. Oral administration involves providing nutrition through the mouth using liquids, powders, or solid foods. Enteral administration refers to providing nutrition through feeding tubes inserted directly into the stomach or intestines. Intravenous administration involves delivering nutrition directly into the bloodstream using intravenous lines. These different administration methods cater to the specific needs and conditions of babies who may have difficulty eating, digesting, or absorbing nutrients orally.</p></p>
