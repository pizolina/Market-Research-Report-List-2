<p><p>Consumer foam is classified into two main types: flexible foam and rigid foam. Flexible foam is soft and pliable, commonly used in cushioning and bedding products such as mattresses and pillows. It provides comfort and support due to its elasticity and ability to conform to the user's body shape. On the other hand, rigid foam is firm and sturdy, often used for insulation purposes or in construction materials. It offers high thermal properties, durability, and structural strength, making it suitable for insulating walls, floors, and roofs in buildings. Both foam types cater to distinct consumer needs and applications.</p></p>
