RNA ASOs work by binding to complementary RNA sequences, preventing their translation into proteins. DNA ASOs, on the other hand, bind to complementary DNA sequences, blocking gene transcription. Both types of ASOs have therapeutic applications in treating genetic disorders and have gained attention in the medical field for their potential to modulate gene expression and provide targeted therapies.</p></p>
