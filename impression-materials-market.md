<p><p>Impression materials refer to substances used in dentistry to create molds or impressions of teeth and oral tissues. There are different types of impression materials available in the market, including alginates, silicones, and polyethers. Alginates are often used for preliminary impressions due to their affordability and ease of use. Silicones are highly accurate and popular for final impressions, while polyethers provide excellent detail reproduction. Each material has its advantages and is chosen based on specific requirements such as accuracy, ease of handling, and cost-effectiveness in different dental procedures.</p></p>
