<p><p>The Refrigerated Centrifuge Market includes two types: Low Speed Refrigerated Centrifuge and High Speed Refrigerated Centrifuge. A Low Speed Refrigerated Centrifuge is designed to separate substances at low speeds, typically ranging from 1,000 to 15,000 revolutions per minute (RPM). It is commonly used in laboratories for basic research and clinical applications. On the other hand, a High Speed Refrigerated Centrifuge is capable of higher speeds, typically exceeding 15,000 RPM, and is used for more advanced applications like DNA sequencing, protein purification, and centrifugation of biological samples. Both types of centrifuges offer temperature control to preserve sample integrity.</p></p>
