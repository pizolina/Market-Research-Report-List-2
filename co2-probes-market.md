<p><p>CO2 probes are devices used to measure carbon dioxide levels in various environments. They come in two main types: embedded and external. Embedded probes are designed to be integrated into a system or equipment, such as inside a building's ventilation system, to continuously monitor CO2 levels. External probes, on the other hand, are standalone devices that can be placed in different areas to measure CO2 levels. They can be moved around based on testing requirements. Both types serve the purpose of assessing and controlling CO2 concentrations for maintenance of healthy and safe environments.</p></p>
