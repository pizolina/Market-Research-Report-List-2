<p><p>Polycythemia vera (PV) is a rare blood disorder characterized by the overproduction of red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets in the bone marrow. It is a chronic condition that requires long-term management to control symptoms and prevent complications. The primary goal of PV treatment is to reduce the risk of blood clots and manage symptoms such as fatigue, itching, and headaches.</p><p>The first-line treatment for PV typically involves phlebotomy, which is the removal of excess blood to keep hematocrit levels within the normal range. After phlebotomy, certain medications, such as hydroxyurea, interferon-alpha, and ruxolitinib, may be prescribed to help regulate blood cell production. In some cases, aspirin or anticoagulant drugs may be used to reduce the risk of clotting.</p><p>The Polycythemia Vera Treatment Market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. These therapies aim to selectively inhibit the mutated genes responsible for the overproduction of blood cells, leading to more effective and safer treatment options.
