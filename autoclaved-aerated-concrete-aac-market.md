<p><p>Autoclaved Aerated Concrete (AAC) is a lightweight building material that usually comes in the form of blocks or panels. It is made from a mixture of natural raw materials such as sand, lime, cement, gypsum, and aluminum powder. During production, the mixture is poured into molds and undergoes a chemical reaction, which produces gas bubbles, resulting in a porous structure. The blocks or panels are then steam-cured under high pressure in an autoclave, hence the name Autoclaved Aerated Concrete.</p><p>The future outlook of the Autoclaved Aerated Concrete (AAC) market is promising, with the market expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 11% during the forecasted period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, AAC offers various advantageous properties such as thermal insulation, fire resistance, durability, and ease of installation, making it increasingly popular for residential and commercial construction. Additionally, the growing focus on sustainable and eco-friendly construction materials is driving the demand for AAC, as it has a reduced environmental impact compared to traditional building materials.</p><p>Furthermore, government initiatives promoting affordable housing and infrastructure development in emerging economies are expected to boost the demand for AAC.
