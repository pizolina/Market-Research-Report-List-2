<p><p>Multi-access laser micromachining refers to the use of lasers to create precise and intricate patterns or structures on a variety of materials. The market is divided into two types: multi-axis low power micromachining and multi-axis high power micromachining. In the low power micromachining market, lasers with lower energy levels are used to perform delicate and detailed work on materials such as polymers or silicon. On the other hand, the high power micromachining market involves the use of lasers with higher energy levels to process harder or thicker materials, like metals or ceramics.</p></p>
