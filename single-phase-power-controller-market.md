<p><p>The single-phase power controller market can be classified into two types: resistive power controller and nonresistive power controller. A resistive power controller is designed to regulate power in devices that solely consume resistive loads, like heaters or incandescent lamps. On the other hand, nonresistive power controllers are used for devices with non-resistive loads, such as motors or transformers. These controllers facilitate power adjustment, enabling efficient control and management of various electrical equipment in different applications.</p></p>
