<p><p>Vanadium Flow Batteries (VFBs) are a type of energy storage technology that use vanadium ions in different oxidation states to store and release electrical energy. These batteries are commonly categorized into three market types: Vanadium Redox Batteries (VRB), Vanadium Electrolyte Symmetric Systems (VESS), and Others. VRBs are characterized by their high energy capacity and long lifespan, making them suitable for large-scale energy storage applications. VESS, on the other hand, have low energy density but offer fast response times and are suitable for power-intensive applications. The "Others" category encompasses different variations and newer developments in VFB technology.</p></p>
