<p><p>An Implantable Venous Access Port (also known as a port-a-cath or implanted port) is a medical device that is surgically implanted under the skin, typically in the chest, to provide long-term access to the venous system. It consists of a small chamber with a silicone septum that is connected to a catheter, which is inserted into a large vein. Implantable Venous Access Ports are commonly used for chemotherapy administration, intravenous medication, and blood transfusions.</p><p>The Implantable Venous Access Port Market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
