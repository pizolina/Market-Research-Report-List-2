<p><p>Rapid Point-of-Care (PoC) diagnostics refer to medical diagnostic tests conducted at or near the site of patient care, providing immediate results for faster treatment decisions. Several factors contribute to this growth, including increasing prevalence of infectious diseases, rising demand for rapid diagnostic tests, and technological advancements in healthcare.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the market is the integration of advanced technologies with PoC diagnostics. The use of miniaturized portable devices and biochips has improved the precision and efficiency of diagnostic tests. Furthermore, the introduction of molecular diagnostics in PoC devices has revolutionized the market by enabling faster detection of diseases such as influenza, HIV, and cancer.</p><p>Additionally, there is a growing focus on developing PoC tests for non-communicable diseases such as diabetes and cardiovascular diseases.
