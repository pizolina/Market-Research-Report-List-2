<p><p>Cardiac implants refer to various devices used to treat cardiac conditions, such as heart failure, arrhythmias, and coronary artery disease. These implants are predominantly used to enhance the heart's functionality, restore normal rhythm, and maintain blood flow. The key cardiac implants include pacemakers, implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICDs), cardiac resynchronization therapy devices (CRTs), left ventricular assist devices (LVADs), and artificial heart valves.</p><p>The cardiac implants market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a steady rate. The market growth can be attributed to factors such as the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases globally, advancements in medical technologies, increasing geriatric population, and lifestyle changes leading to unhealthy habits. Additionally, the growing demand for minimally invasive procedures and the rising adoption of implantable medical devices further drive market growth.</p><p>Emerging trends in the cardiac implants market include the development of leadless pacemakers, smartphone-based monitoring systems, and the use of artificial intelligence (AI) in cardiac implants. Leadless pacemakers offer a wireless and battery-powered alternative to conventional pacemakers, providing improved patient outcomes and reducing complications associated with traditional pacing systems. Smartphone-based monitoring systems allow patients to transmit data from their implanted devices to healthcare professionals remotely, improving patient care and reducing hospital visits. The integration of AI into cardiac implants enables personalized treatment plans and real-time monitoring of patients' cardiac conditions.</p><p>In conclusion, the cardiac implants market is expected to exhibit robust growth in the coming years due to the increasing burden of cardiovascular diseases and technological advancements in cardiac implants. The market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 8.9% during the forecast period, driven by factors such as the rising adoption of implantable medical devices and the development of innovative cardiac implant technologies.</p></p>
