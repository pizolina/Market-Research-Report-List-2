<p><p>Laser beam collimation refers to the process of making a laser beam parallel or focused. Reflective collimators utilize mirrors to redirect the laser beam, ensuring it is collimated. This type is commonly used in applications requiring high power lasers. Refractive collimators, on the other hand, use lenses to achieve collimation. They are typically used with low-power lasers and offer a cost-effective solution. Both reflective and refractive collimators play crucial roles in various industries such as telecommunications, medical devices, and scientific research.</p></p>
