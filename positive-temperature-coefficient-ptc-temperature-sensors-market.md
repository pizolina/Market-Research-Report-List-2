<p><p>Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) temperature sensors are devices that have a resistance value which increases with rising temperature. There are two types of PTC temperature sensors in the market: CPTC and PPTC temperature sensors. CPTC sensors have a ceramic material that exhibits a positive temperature coefficient, causing their resistance to increase with temperature. PPTC sensors, on the other hand, utilize a polymer material that undergoes a reversible phase change at a specific temperature, causing a sharp increase in resistance. Both types of sensors are widely used in applications that require temperature monitoring and control.</p></p>
