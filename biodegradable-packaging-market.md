<p><p>The biodegradable packaging market can be divided into two types: natural and synthetic. Natural biodegradable packaging refers to packaging materials that are derived from natural sources, such as plants and animals. These materials can easily decompose in the environment, leaving minimal or no harmful residue behind. On the other hand, synthetic biodegradable packaging is made from synthetic materials, such as polymers, that are designed to break down into smaller, non-toxic components over time. Both types of biodegradable packaging aim to reduce waste and environmental impact by offering sustainable alternatives to traditional packaging materials.</p></p>
