<p><p>Industrial microfiltration refers to the use of filtration techniques in industries to separate particles and impurities from various substances. The market includes three main types: air microfiltration, liquid microfiltration, and oil microfiltration. </p><p>Air microfiltration involves the removal of contaminants and particles from the air in industrial processes, ensuring clean and safe working environments. </p><p>Liquid microfiltration is used to purify liquids by removing suspended solids, bacteria, and other impurities. It is widely applied in industries such as food and beverage, pharmaceuticals, and water treatment. </p><p>Oil microfiltration aims to eliminate contaminants from oil, improving its quality and extending its usability in lubrication, hydraulic systems, and other applications.</p></p>
