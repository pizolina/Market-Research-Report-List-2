<p><p>Seawater electrochlorination systems are used for the disinfection or treatment of seawater by the electrolysis process. These systems utilize the principle of electrolysis to convert seawater into a solution of sodium hypochlorite, which acts as a powerful disinfectant and biocide. The electrochlorination process is commonly used in marine applications such as desalination plants, ballast water treatment, and cooling systems.</p><p>The market for seawater electrochlorination systems is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. Factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for clean and safe water in various industries such as power generation, oil & gas, and shipping. Additionally, the rising concerns about the environmental impact of traditional chemical disinfection methods have led to the adoption of electrochlorination systems as a more sustainable and eco-friendly alternative.</p><p>Moreover, stringent regulations imposed by environmental agencies on ballast water management and the treatment of wastewater from desalination plants have fueled the demand for seawater electrochlorination systems.
