<p><p>Food encapsulation of new active ingredients includes various market types such as microencapsulation, nanoencapsulation, and hybrid encapsulation. Microencapsulation involves enclosing tiny particles of the active ingredient in a protective coating to enhance stability and controlled release. Nanoencapsulation utilizes nanoparticles to encapsulate the ingredient, allowing for better solubility and targeted delivery. Hybrid encapsulation combines the advantages of both microencapsulation and nanoencapsulation to achieve optimal protection, solubility, and controlled release of the active ingredient. These techniques play a crucial role in improving the effectiveness and functionality of food products.</p></p>
